About Me

My journey in psychology began with a deep curiosity about human development and emotional wellbeing. Transitioning from a B.Sc. in Psychology to an M.Sc. in Educational Psychology, I have cultivated a strong professional identity centered around inclusive education and child development.

I passionately believe that children require individualized, emotionally safe learning environments supported by structured interventions. My work is driven by the commitment to transform traditional educational paradigms into inclusive spaces where every child feels seen, understood, and supported in their unique developmental journey.

Philosophy of Practice

I believe that emotional safety is the absolute foundation for any meaningful learning. In my practice, I view behaviour as communication, prioritizing understanding over punishment. I advocate for structured, proactive intervention rather than reactive measures, deeply rooted in the belief that inclusion is not a special circumstance, but a baseline educational principle every child deserves.
